About Lagora

A community marketplace born in Laguna, built for Laguna.

🌿

The Story Behind Lagora

Lagora was born from a simple observation: the small entrepreneurs, artisans, and service providers of Pagsanjan and Sta. Cruz, Laguna were being left behind by the big online platforms.

Large marketplaces like Shopee and Lazada favor high-volume sellers with big marketing budgets. Local sellers were resorting to informal social media channels — fragmented, unsafe, and hard to manage.

We believed there was a better way. A marketplace designed specifically for local communities, where trust is built in, fees are fair, and every transaction supports a neighbor.

The name Lagora combines "Laguna" — the geographic heart of this community — and "Agora" — the ancient Greek public marketplace that was a center of commerce, dialogue, and civic life. Lagora is exactly that: a modern digital agora for Laguna.

Our Mission

"To empower local entrepreneurs and strengthen community-based commerce through a secure, structured, and inclusive digital marketplace."

Every feature of Lagora is built with one question in mind: Does this help our local community thrive? From the verified seller system to the transparent fee structure, Lagora puts community first.

Academic Foundation

Lagora is grounded in three theoretical frameworks

Theory 1

Platform Theory

Parker, Van Alstyne & Choudary (2016) — Digital platforms create value through network effects. As more users join, the platform becomes more valuable for everyone.

Theory 2

Technology Acceptance Model

Davis (1989) — Users adopt technology based on perceived usefulness and ease of use. Lagora is designed to be simple, intuitive, and genuinely helpful for local sellers.

Theory 3

Financial Feasibility Theory

Brigham & Ehrhardt (2017) — Every venture needs systematic financial evaluation: costs, revenues, break-even analysis, and ROI. Lagora is built to be self-sustaining.

Lagora was developed as a feasibility study for a Bachelor of Science in Business Administration (Major in Financial Management) at Laguna State Polytechnic University, Main Campus, Sta. Cruz, Laguna. February 2026.
